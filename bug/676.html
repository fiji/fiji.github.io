After I finished the segmentation part of different structures of the neuropile, often the saving did not occur. No saving in the working directory, except the image in stack. But no label saved. Once, it worked (without any special different prodecure), the label was saved and the second stack came in the window. But here again, after finishing the segmentation job, the saving did not occur.

 I tried several times, but I really have no clue for this issue. A guy and me tried to do exactly the same procedure in pairs : it worked for me and not for him.

If you could shed a light on this, it would be very kind. I don't know if it is possible that I missed an update or something like it. Is the VIB protocol still updated and available? Do you also have this kind of problem?

Thank you for your attention

Comment 1 Curtis Rueden 2014-06-16 15:19:10 CDT 
We try to keep plugins like the VIB Protocol working, but its main author, Bene Schmid, has not been active in the Fiji community for a couple of years now.

The symptoms you are describing (where two people do the exact same thing with the exact same data and get different results) sound like a race condition, which would definitely be a bug in the code. However, without a small reproducible test case, it becomes very difficult to diagnose the issue and there is little we can do to assist.

If you find a way to reliably reproduce the problem, please follow up with more details. Otherwise, we'll probably have to close this issue without a fix.

There is one thing I notice: your update check says "PROTECTED_LOCATION" which means you have installed Fiji into "C:\Program Files" which is highly discouraged. I know: it's very unintuitive. But the fact is that Windows will not grant Fiji permission to update itself if it is installed into Program Files -- blame Microsoft.

So you could try with your Fiji installed to e.g. C:\Users\mbenz\Programs\Fiji.app (or wherever your user directory is), with a fully updated (or freshly downloaded) copy of Fiji, and see if you still have the same problems.
